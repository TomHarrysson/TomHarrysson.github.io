Clinically Significant Anatomical Variants

A concise, system-based overview of important anatomical variants with clinical and surgical relevance. Source: Bergman’s Encyclopedia of Human Anatomic Variation, Radiopaedia, and major anatomy texts.

CARDIOVASCULAR SYSTEM

Cardiac Structural Variants
VariantPrevalenceClinical significance
Patent Foramen Ovale (PFO) ~25% Allows potential for paradoxical embolism (→ stroke); associated with migraine and decompression sickness.
Coronary Artery Variants
VariantPrevalenceClinical significance
Left-dominant coronary circulation ~10% Alters infarct territory and surgical bypass strategy. (Right-dominant is ~80-85%).
Anomalous coronary origin (e.g., RCA from left sinus) <1% May have malignant interarterial courses → sudden cardiac death risk; important for surgical correction.
Separate LAD and LCx origins from left sinus ~1–2% Can complicate catheterization and graft planning.
Aortic Arch & Great Vessel Variants
VariantPrevalenceClinical significance
“Bovine” arch (common origin of brachiocephalic & left common carotid) ~15–25% Common variant; relevant in carotid stenting and thoracic surgery access planning.
Aberrant right subclavian artery (arteria lusoria) ~0.5–2% May cause dysphagia lusoria; important to know for mediastinal surgery and central line routes.
Right aortic arch / double aortic arch Rare Can compress trachea or esophagus (vascular ring); often congenital associations.
Cerebral & Head/Neck Vascular Variants
VariantPrevalenceClinical significance
Incomplete Circle of Willis (e.g., PCoA hypoplasia) ~25–50% Reduces collateral flow; influences stroke patterns and aneurysm risk.
Fetal-type posterior cerebral artery (fetal PCA) ~10–30% PCA originates from ICA; alters perfusion territories; important for endovascular procedures.
Aberrant internal carotid artery in middle ear Rare Pulsatile middle ear mass; major risk if unrecognized during ENT surgery.
Persistent trigeminal / hypoglossal artery <1% Embryologic carotid–basilar anastomoses that complicate angiography.
Hypoplastic transverse/sigmoid sinus ~20% Can mimic venous occlusion (thrombosis) on imaging; clinical correlation required.
Thoracic Venous Variants
VariantPrevalenceClinical significance
Common ostium of left pulmonary veins ~20% Key in atrial fibrillation ablation mapping and catheter approach.
Supernumerary right middle pulmonary vein ~25–30% May require identification during pulmonary vein isolation/ablation.
Azygos continuation of IVC ~0.5% Incidental on CT; changes venous return mapping and alters IVC filter considerations.
Abdominal Arterial Variants
VariantPrevalenceClinical significance
Replaced right hepatic artery from SMA (Michels Type III) ~15% Crucial for hepatic resection, donor hepatectomy, and cholecystectomy approach.
Replaced left hepatic artery from left gastric (Michels Type II) ~10% Important for gastric and hepatic surgery; at risk in gastrectomy.
Accessory renal arteries ~25–30% Important in nephrectomy, transplant planning, and renal angiography.
Celiacomesenteric trunk ~1% Rare variant; important for upper abdominal vascular surgery and endovascular planning.
Abdominal Venous Variants
VariantPrevalenceClinical significance
Retroaortic left renal vein 2–4% Can alter approach in aortic and renal surgery; risk during AAA repair.
Circumaortic left renal vein ~8% May complicate adrenal or renal operations; watch for unexpected venous channels.
Portal vein trifurcation ~10–20% Affects segmental hepatic resection and transplant planning.
Duplicated gonadal (testicular/ovarian) veins <5% May influence varicocele pathology and embolization procedures.
Limb Vascular Variants
VariantPrevalenceClinical significance
High bifurcation of brachial artery 10–20% Risk during venipuncture and invasive access; alters pulse examination anatomy.
Superficial ulnar artery ~5% May be injured during venepuncture; mistaken for a vein.
Persistent sciatic artery <0.05% Can form aneurysms; important in peripheral angiography and bypass planning.

References & notes

Prevalence estimates are approximate and vary by population and imaging modality. Use imaging or intraoperative assessment to confirm anatomy prior to intervention.

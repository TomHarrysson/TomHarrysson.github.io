  • Unlike other similar bleeding risk scores, this tool was specifically developed to assess the risk associated with DOAC therapy, rather than with vitamin K antagonists.
  • The risk score was primarily validated for severe bleeding. Clinicians should also consider the risks and quality of life impacts of other bleeding outcomes in their discussions.
  • This tool does not cover all potential risks for bleeding; therefore, it should be used alongside clinical reasoning.
  • Initially performed better than the HAS-BLED score, though further research is necessary for confirmation.
Advice
  • The decision to use anticoagulation therapy with a DOAC should consider multiple factors. This tool should not be the sole determinant of treatment planning, but should guide shared decision-making between the patient and clinician.
  • A high score is not necessarily a contraindication to DOAC therapy; clinical context is key.
  • Where possible, efforts should be made to address modifiable risk factors for bleeding.
